# Record Citation Extraction — Research Spike Findings

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Issue:** #74

## Overview

Record citations reference documents within a case's own record rather than external legal authority. They appear in appellate briefs, trial briefs, summary judgment motions, and other litigation filings. Unlike authority citations (cases, statutes), record citations are **not shepherdized** — they point to the trial record, not published law.

This spike catalogs the forms found across federal and state practice, assesses frequency and collision risk, and recommends a phased approach.

---

## Catalog of Record Citation Forms

### Category 1: Appellate Record Citations (Highest Frequency)

These appear in virtually every appellate brief.

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Format Examples | Notes |

## Collision Analysis

### High Risk — Will produce false positives without careful handling

| Abbreviation | Collision With | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| `A.` | Atlantic Reporter (`A.`, `A.2d`, `A.3d`), person initials, article references | **Critical** — `A.` alone is ambiguous without context. `A.2d` / `A.3d` are case reporters. Requires disambiguation. |
| `Ex.` | "Example", "Exercise", sentence-initial "Ex." | **High** — very common word. Needs trailing number/letter. |
| `R.` | Sentence-initial abbreviation, "R." as an initial | **Medium** — requires "at" or page number to disambiguate. |
| `Doc.` | "Doctor", "Document" in non-citation context | **Medium** — needs trailing number. |
| `Br.` | "Brother", British abbreviation | **Low-Medium** — legal context makes this less ambiguous. |
| `CR` / `RR` | Varies (CR = credit, criminal record; RR = railroad) | **Low** — Texas-only, numeric context helps. |

### Low Risk — Distinctive enough to extract reliably

| Abbreviation | Why Low Risk |
|---|---|
| `J.A.` / `JA` | Distinctive abbreviation + numeric page |
| `Pet. App.` | Two-word compound is distinctive |
| `ECF No.` / `Dkt. No.` | Keyword + "No." pattern |
| `Compl. ¶` | Abbreviation + paragraph symbol |
| `Dep.` + name | Name prefix provides context |
| `Tr.` + page:line | Page:line format is distinctive |
| `Hr'g Tr.` | Apostrophe contraction is distinctive |

## Structural Observations

### How Record Citations Differ from Authority Citations

1. **No volume-reporter-page triple.** Record citations don't follow the `VOL REPORTER PAGE` pattern that case citations use.
2. **Pinpoint structure varies by type:**
   - Page only: `R. at 45`, `J.A. 200`
   - Page:line: `Tr. 50:1-15`, `Dep. 13:5`
   - Paragraph: `Compl. ¶ 10`, `Aff. ¶¶ 2-3`
   - Entry number: `Dkt. No. 45`, `ECF No. 12`
   - Letter/number designation: `Ex. A`, `Ex. 5`
3. **Optional name prefix.** Depositions, affidavits, declarations, and exhibits often carry the name of the person/party: `Smith Dep.`, `Jones Aff.`, `Pl. Ex.`.
4. **Optional party prefix.** Many forms can be prefixed with party designations: `Def.`, `Pl.`, `Pet.`, `Resp.`, `Gov't`.
5. **Parenthetical wrapping is optional.** `(R. at 45.)` and `R. at 45` are both valid.
6. **Page suffix letters.** Appendix citations may use letter suffixes: `12a`, `32a`.

### Taxonomy

Record citations cluster into three structural families:

1. **Page-reference types** — `ABBREV [at] PAGE[suffix]` — R., J.A., App., Pet. App., S.A., Br., CR, RR, CT, RT
2. **Paragraph-reference types** — `[NAME] ABBREV ¶[¶] NUM[-NUM]` — Compl., Aff., Decl., Stip., SMF, Interrog.
3. **Entry-reference types** — `ABBREV [No.] NUM[-NUM] [at PAGE]` — Dkt., ECF, Doc., Ex., DE

## Frequency Assessment (Rough Priority)

Based on prevalence in federal and state practice:

| Tier | Forms | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** (nearly universal) | `R. at`, `Tr.`, `J.A.`, `Dkt.`/`ECF No.`, `Ex.`, `Compl.`, `Aff.`/`Decl.` | Found in most briefs across jurisdictions |
| **Tier 2** (very common) | `Pet. App.`, `App.`, `Dep.`, `Br.`, `Mot.`/`Opp'n`/`Reply`/`Mem.`, `A.`, `S.A.` | Common in specific practice areas |
| **Tier 3** (jurisdiction-specific) | `CR`/`RR` (TX), `CT`/`RT` (CA), `Hr'g Tr.`, `Sent. Tr.`, `Plea Tr.` | State-specific or criminal practice |
| **Tier 4** (niche) | `SMF`/`SOMF`, `RSMF`, `Interrog.`, `RFA`, `GX`/`PX`/`DX`, `Addend.`, `ALCR` | Summary judgment practice, specialized |

## Open Questions for Design Phase

1. **Disambiguation strategy for `A.`** — Should we require `A.` + bare number (no series like `2d`, `3d`) to distinguish from Atlantic Reporter? Or skip `A.` entirely and only support `App.`?
2. **Name extraction** — Should we attempt to extract the declarant/deponent name from `Smith Dep. 45:3`? Or just flag the record type and pinpoint?
3. **Party prefix handling** — Should `Def. Mot. 12` be a single record citation, or should we only match the core `Mot. 12` and ignore party prefixes?
4. **Confidence scoring** — Ambiguous forms (`R.`, `A.`, `Ex.`) should get lower confidence. What thresholds?
5. **Scope boundary** — Should record citations participate in resolution (linking short forms to full forms)? Or are they standalone?
6. **State-specific opt-in** — Should Texas/California record forms (`CR`, `RR`, `CT`, `RT`) require a jurisdiction flag, or always be active?
