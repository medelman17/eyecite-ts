# Provenance & Data Lineage for Multi-Hop Quoting Chains (Id./supra Attribution)

**Date:** 2026-06-02
**Query:** What is the minimal, verifiable data model for representing a multi-hop legal quoting chain ("language quoted in Hogue, originally from Weinreb, surfaced via …")? Is the provenance-semiring algebra warranted, or is a typed PROV-style DAG enough? How is per-hop verification modeled? How should a citation-provenance graph relate to a downstream citation/proposition graph, if the consuming application maintains one?
**Depth:** deep

## Summary

For this problem, **a typed DAG is the right data model and the provenance-semiring algebra is overkill** — but the semiring literature is still worth reading because it tells us *why*. The quoting chain we want to record ("Weinreb → quoted-in Corsello → quoted-in Hogue") is a small, acyclic, mostly-linear derivation graph, and W3C PROV-DM already ships a purpose-built vocabulary for exactly this: `prov:wasQuotedFrom` is defined as "the repeat of (some or all of) an entity, such as text … by someone who may or may not be its original author." The semiring framework (Green–Karvounarakis–Tannen, PODS'07) earns its complexity only when you need to *propagate a value* (trust, probability, cost) through a relational query with `+` for alternative derivations and `×` for joint derivations; our chain has neither alternative-use fan-in nor joint-use, so a polynomial buys nothing a labeled edge doesn't. The provenance graph should be **attribution metadata hung on a downstream citation/proposition graph (if the consumer maintains one), not a parallel store** — model each quoting hop as a reified, per-hop-verifiable derivation edge between the same authority nodes that graph already has. Critically, provenance is the **audit/explanation layer (stage 5 of the pipeline), not the resolver** — recording the chain does not by itself fix the Hogue/Corsello bug; scope (stage 2) does.

## Key Findings

### 1. W3C PROV-DM has a native, exactly-fit vocabulary for quoting chains
PROV-DM (W3C Recommendation, 30 April 2013) defines three core types — **Entity** ("a physical, digital, conceptual … thing"), **Activity**, **Agent** — and a derivation relation `wasDerivedFrom`. Quotation, revision, and primary-source are *kinds* of derivation in PROV-DM; their named-property form — `wasQuotedFrom` / `wasRevisionOf` / `hadPrimarySource` — is defined in PROV-O (the ontology):
- **`wasQuotedFrom`**: "the repeat of (some or all of) an entity, such as text or image, by someone who may or may not be its original author." This is *exactly* the legal quoting relation.
- **`wasRevisionOf`**: "a derivation for which the resulting entity is a revised version of some original."
- **`hadPrimarySource`**: cites "something produced by some agent with direct experience and knowledge about the topic" — i.e., the *originating* authority at the end of the chain (Weinreb).

So "language quoted in Hogue, originally from Weinreb" maps directly: `Hogue_quote wasQuotedFrom Corsello_quote`, `Corsello_quote wasQuotedFrom Weinreb_passage`, and `Weinreb_passage hadPrimarySource Weinreb`. PROV is RDF/OWL (PROV-O, also a 30 April 2013 Recommendation), so these are just triples that "integrate seamlessly into existing semantic-web graphs without requiring separate data silos."

### 2. PROV derivation requires a chain but is NOT axiomatically transitive — record each hop explicitly
PROV-DM states "a chain of usage and generation is necessary for a derivation to hold between entities," but the spec does **not** make `wasDerivedFrom` transitive. Practically: do not store only the endpoints (Hogue→Weinreb) and infer the middle; **store every hop as its own edge** (Hogue→Corsello, Corsello→Weinreb). This is what gives you per-hop verifiability and lets you surface partial chains when one hop can't be confirmed. PROV also distinguishes *imprecise* derivation (two entities, no detail) from *precise* derivation, where "the generation and usage of the generated and used entities … can be provided" — the precise form is where per-hop evidence attaches.

### 3. The semiring framework: what `+` and `×` actually buy you
Green, Karvounarakis & Tannen, **"Provenance Semirings," PODS'07** (Beijing, 11–14 June 2007, pp. 31–40), unify incomplete/probabilistic/bag/why-provenance as a single algorithm over a **commutative semiring** `(K, +, ·, 0, 1)`. The operations have a precise provenance meaning (their Definition 3.2):
- **`+` (union / projection)** combines tags of the *same* output produced **alternatively** — i.e., this result can be obtained *this way OR that way*.
- **`·` (join)** combines tags of *joinable* inputs used **jointly** — i.e., this result needs *this AND that*.

The paper proves (Prop. 3.4) that the expected relational-algebra identities **force** `K` to be a commutative semiring — the algebra isn't arbitrary. The **provenance polynomial semiring `N[X]`** (Definition 4.1: polynomials with `N` coefficients over tuple-id variables `X`) is the "most general" annotation: their **Proposition 4.2** gives, for any semiring `K` and valuation `v: X→K`, a *unique* homomorphism `Eval_v: N[X]→K`. **This is the load-bearing result**: compute provenance once as a polynomial, then *evaluate* it into any specific semiring — Boolean (set existence), `N` (multiplicity/bag), `[0,1]`-style (probability/confidence), or a security lattice (trust/clearance) — to get trust, probability, or cost "for free." Example from the paper: a tuple's provenance `2s² + rs` reads as "computed three ways: two use input `s` twice, one uses `r` and `s`."

### 4. …but that machinery is keyed to *query-driven* derivation with fan-in, which our chain lacks
The semiring payoff requires (a) a relational/Datalog query whose operators you propagate through, and (b) genuine `+`-style alternative derivations and `×`-style joint derivations to fold into a value. A linear quoting chain (A quotes B quotes C) is a **path**, not a query result: no alternative-use disjunction, no joint-use conjunction. Evaluating a polynomial that is just `a·b·c` (or a single variable per hop) collapses to "did every hop hold?" — which a typed DAG answers directly by walking edges. **Verdict: the semiring algebra is the wrong tool for *representing + verifying* a chain.** It would only become relevant if we later wanted to compute a *confidence score for a proposition supported by multiple, partially-overlapping authority chains* (`+` over alternative supporting chains, `×` along each chain) — that is a proposition-graph aggregation problem (see #7), not a citation-attribution one.

### 5. Why/Where/How provenance — the distinction tells us which question we're answering
Buneman, Khanna & Tan, **"Why and Where: A Characterization of Data Provenance," ICDT 2001** (pp. 316–330), introduced the split, verbatim:
- **Why-provenance**: "why is it in the database" — the set of source tuples that *contributed to / influenced the existence* of the output (the witness set).
- **Where-provenance**: "where does a given piece of data come from" — "the location(s) in the source databases from which the data was extracted."

Cheney, Chiticariu & Tan, **"Provenance in Databases: Why, How, and Where," Foundations and Trends in Databases, Vol. 1, No. 4 (2007), pp. 379–474** (©2009) synthesize three notions, with **how-provenance (the semiring polynomial) being strictly the most informative** — it refines why-provenance by also recording *multiplicity and the structure of the derivation*, and the survey notes where-provenance is "contained in" why-provenance. **Mapping to us:** a legal quoting chain is fundamentally a **where/how** question ("this text was *copied from* that passage, via these intermediate opinions") — *not* a why-question. We care about the **copy locations and the path**, which is precisely the where + structural-how axis. This is another reason PROV's `wasQuotedFrom` (a copy/repeat relation) fits better than a contribution-set model.

### 6. Per-hop verification: two complementary mechanisms (attribute the edge; content-address the node)
Frameworks model per-step verification two ways, and we can use both:
- **Attribute the derivation edge.** PROV lets every relation carry attributes. The reference implementation `trungdong/prov` (Python W3C PROV library, in-memory model with PROV-O/XML/JSON serialization) exposes `quotation(generatedEntity, usedEntity, …, other_attributes=…)` and `wasDerivedFrom(…, attributes=…)`; every relation accepts an `other_attributes` dict, so a single hop can record `verified: true|flagged`, `method: eyecite-isParentheticalChild`, `confidence: 0.9`, `verifierActivity: <run-id>`. In RDF this is the **qualified-relation pattern**: reify the edge as a `prov:Derivation` node (`prov:entity`, `prov:hadActivity`) so attributes attach to *that one hop* rather than the endpoints.
- **Content-address the node (cryptographic hop verification).** A **Merkle DAG** (IPFS/IPLD) gives each node an ID that is "the result of hashing the node's contents … and the list of identifiers of its children." Verification becomes recomputation: a Merkle DAG is "a DAG where each node has an identifier … the result of hashing the node's contents … and the list of identifiers of its children," and Merkle DAGs are "self-verified structures" — any change to a node alters its identifier and thus every ancestor's. For us, hashing each quoted span (normalized text) makes a hop *self-verifying*: if the stored span-hash no longer matches the document text the quote was anchored to, that hop is automatically `flagged` — a natural feed into the pipeline's ABSTAIN gate. This is a fail-closed posture: store a stable content-hash over the normalized span text and recompute-and-compare on read.

### 7. Don't build a parallel lineage store — reuse the proposition graph
The proposition-graph already links legal propositions to supporting authorities; its authority nodes (cases, passages) are the *same* entities a quoting chain connects. PROV is designed to layer onto an existing graph (RDF triples; "provenance of provenance" via **bundles** — "a named set of provenance descriptions, and is itself an entity"). Concrete guidance:
- **One node namespace.** A quoted *passage*/*span* is an Entity that `specializationOf` / is part of an authority node already in the proposition graph (Hogue, Corsello, Weinreb). Do not mint duplicate case nodes.
- **Quoting chain = typed edges among those nodes.** Add `quotedFrom` / `originatesFrom` edges (mirroring `prov:wasQuotedFrom` / `prov:hadPrimarySource`) between span nodes. The chain is a sub-DAG of the proposition graph, not a separate database.
- **The resolved antecedent of an Id./supra is itself a provenance fact.** "This `Id.` resolves to Hogue" is `wasDerivedFrom`-style attribution: record it as an edge with the deciding `Activity` (the resolver run) and its `other_attributes` (scope-filter applied? salience score? abstained?). This makes stage-4 ABSTAIN decisions auditable in the same store.
- **Keep aggregation (semiring-style) at the proposition layer, if ever.** If product later wants "how confident are we this proposition is supported," that's where `+`/`×` over multiple authority chains could live — *above* the citation-attribution layer, not inside it.

## How This Applies to Id./supra Attribution

Tie each finding to the staged pipeline and the canonical bug
(`"Hogue v. State, 123 U.S. 1 (2020) (quoting Corsello v. Verizon, 456 N.Y. 2 (2010))." … Id.` must resolve to **Hogue**, not the parenthetical-buried **Corsello**):

- **Stage 5 (PROVENANCE) is what this slice delivers — and it is an explanation layer, not the fix.** Recording `Hogue_span wasQuotedFrom Corsello_span` and `Corsello_span hadPrimarySource Weinreb` gives the "language quoted in Hogue, originally from Weinreb" chain verbatim, using PROV's native quotation/primary-source relations (#1). But **the chain does not resolve the `Id.`** — it presupposes the scope decision. The fix for the bug is stage 2 (SCOPE: parenthetical-internal Corsello is invisible to the outer `Id.`); provenance then *records and explains* the chosen resolution and the inner→outer quoting relationship.
- **Use a typed DAG, not the semiring algebra (#3, #4).** The quoting chain is a path with no alternative-use or joint-use fan-in, so a provenance polynomial degenerates to "every hop held?" — exactly what walking typed edges answers. Adopt PROV-DM's `wasQuotedFrom`/`hadPrimarySource`/`wasDerivedFrom`; skip `N[X]`.
- **This is where/how-provenance, not why (#5).** We're tracking *copied text and its path*, which is the where + structural-how axis — another reason the contribution-set ("why") models and the witness-basis machinery don't fit, and the copy-relation `wasQuotedFrom` does.
- **Per-hop verification feeds the ABSTAIN gate (#6, stage 4).** Attach `verified|flagged` + method + confidence to *each* derivation edge (PROV `other_attributes` / qualified `prov:Derivation`). Content-address each quoted span (Merkle-style hash of normalized text); a hash mismatch auto-flags that hop. When any hop is `flagged` or the bracket structure (stage 1, the stack parser) couldn't be recovered, the chain is incomplete → **flag for review, never silently fall back to recency.** Record the abstention itself as a provenance fact.
- **Store it in the consumer's graph (#7).** The resolver's output ("this `Id.` → Hogue") is a `wasDerivedFrom` edge carrying the deciding run as its `Activity`; the quoting chain is a sub-DAG over authority nodes that graph already owns. No parallel lineage store. This also means stage-2 scope and stage-3 salience decisions become *queryable, explainable* edges — directly supporting any "show/explain the resolution" surface.

## Trade-offs & Alternatives

| Option | Pros | Cons | Verdict for us |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Typed DAG (PROV-DM `wasQuotedFrom` etc.), reified per-hop edges** | Native fit; per-hop attributes; embeds in proposition graph; auditable | Must store every hop (no free transitivity) | **Recommended.** Minimal model that satisfies represent + verify each hop. |
| **Provenance semiring `N[X]` (how-provenance polynomials)** | Compute once, evaluate to trust/prob/cost via one homomorphism (Prop 4.2); rigorous | Keyed to relational/Datalog queries with `+`/`×` fan-in we don't have; heavy | **Overkill** for a chain. Revisit only for proposition-level multi-chain confidence aggregation. |
| **Content-addressed Merkle DAG (IPLD-style)** | Cryptographic, recomputable per-hop verification; tamper-evident; fail-closed | Immutability/CID churn; not a relationship vocabulary on its own | **Adopt the hashing idea** for span verification; pair with PROV for the relations. |
| **Why-provenance / witness-basis (Buneman; Cui–Widom–Wiener lineage)** | Mature contribution-tracking | Answers "why in DB," a *set*; loses copy-location + path | **Wrong axis** (we need where/how, not why). |
| **RDF-star / named graphs / nanopublications for statement-level provenance** | Standard ways to attach source/certainty to a single triple; nanopubs = assertion+provenance+pubinfo as named graphs | RDF reification "rarely used" in practice (survey found 5/72 endpoints); tooling weight | Viable *if* the proposition graph is already RDF; otherwise the in-graph reified-edge pattern is lighter. |

## Open Questions

1. **What is the proposition graph's substrate?** If it is RDF/property-graph, PROV-O triples drop in directly and the qualified-relation pattern is idiomatic; if it is relational/typed tables, the "reified derivation edge" becomes a typed `quoting_hop` table (from_span, to_span, kind ∈ {quoted_from, primary_source}, verified, method, confidence, activity_run_id). Which substrate decides the concrete schema.
2. **Span identity / coordinate space.** Per-hop hashing needs a stable normalized-text key for each quoted span across whatever coordinate spaces the consumer juggles (e.g. markdown / rendered / flattened editor DOM). Use one stable content-hash (e.g. FNV-1a-64 over NFC-normalized text) rather than inventing a second hashing scheme.
3. **How deep do chains realistically go, and where does the chain terminate?** "(quoting X (quoting Y))" nesting in real briefs — is 2–3 hops the practical ceiling? Terminal node = `hadPrimarySource` (a secondary-source treatise like Weinreb) vs. another case. Affects whether depth limits or cycle guards are needed.
4. **Is multi-chain confidence aggregation ever a product requirement?** Only if "how strongly is this proposition supported by overlapping authority chains" becomes a feature does the semiring `+`/`×` evaluation earn its keep — and at the proposition layer, not citation attribution.
